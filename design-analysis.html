Design Principles

Proximity —

ESPN.com makes good use of proximity. The principle of proximity says that like items should be close and visibly separated from unalike items. ESPN does a nice job of grouping similar items together by sport, topic, as well as other criteria. As you can see in this picture, the main method they use to group is the boxes that the groups are contained in. They are subtle. It is a slight color difference from the background, but it helps the mind group like things together in a wonderful way. Alike items are close to each other in the boxes and the boxes provide the break for items that are different.

Alignment —

ESPN.com also does a superb job in applying the principle of alignment. Alignment creates structure and balance in a website. As you can see in the following screenshot, there is a strong left alignment with the logos for the different categories. This provides a structure for the viewer to know that each time one sees a logo left aligned in that matter, then a new topic is being viewed. The logo is not the only consistent alignment in the screenshot. The headers for each section and the body text are aligned at the same place. These strong alignments are visually appealing and will help the viewer have a better experience.
